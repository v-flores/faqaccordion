COFFEE FAQ


Welcome to Victor's FAQ on Coffee. Below are some commonly asked coffee-related questions.

Is coffee dehydrating?

+

Drinking caffeinated beverages does not cause fluid loss. Coffee can actually be just as hydrating as water. The diuretic effect of caffeine is not strong enough to counter-balance the benefits of fluid intake from drinking coffee.

Does coffee improve levels of concentration?

+

Brain activity that controls memory and concentration is temporarily boosted. This can cause an increase in energy and alertness. What a great way to stay awake and focused at work!

Is drinking coffee good for your health?

+

A coffee a day can keep the doctor away! Coffee is full of antioxidants that helps cleanse toxins from our bodies especially those that cause disease.

Fun Fact: People who drink four or more cups of coffee daily have a 50 percent lower risk of Type 2 diabetes.

How does coffee keep us awake?

+

Caffeine! It is a stimulant that boosts mental performance positively enough for studying, working, or completing complex tasks. Just remember serotonin is drained throughout the caffeine experience, eventually making for the coffee high to wear away. This is where that third or fourth cup comes in handy!

Is coffee bad for your heart?

+

Studies have shown that 2 cups of coffee per day can actually help reduce the risk of heart disease. Drinking the equivalent of two cups of coffee per day appears to have the most significant benefit on heart health, when compared to no coffee consumption. The American Heart Association recommends that heart failure patients consume no more than a cup or two of coffee a day.

When is the best time to drink coffee?

+

If you find yourself needing more than your normal amount of caffeine, try drinking your coffee between 9:30 a.m. and 11:30 a.m. This way the caffeine won’t be suppressed from your body’s natural caffeinated jolt.
